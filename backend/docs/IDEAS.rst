----------------------------------
Spare Transcendence project ideas
----------------------------------

Throwaway / brainstormed project ideas. Maybe they can be used one day, but probably not today.

* Letterboxd, but federated.

* Message in a bottle.
  You leave a random message (text, audio, picture) for some random person. You pick up any random message.

* Exquisite Corpse (what was the British name for this?)
  Write a line (or larger part) of a story. It gets passed to another person, who sees only a part,  and they write another part. It gets passed on again, for some amount of times. Finally the whole story is revealed, to general acclaim and amusement (or not). Rate, save the best stories.

* Doomscrolling defeated.
  Use an endless scrolling / flick away UI to dispay material that is Actually Useful, e.g. wikipedia articles or poems.

* Rap Battle / vocab fight
  Take pairs of words that mean basically the same in two different languages. The user chooses the one they like best, based on their subjective criteria. Potential features include the import of Anki vocabulary decks, stats display, replay texts altered based on the results in a rap battle style (think Beastie Boys meme where they SHOUT random INTERRUPTIONS over EL OTRO).

* Video Sharing, but anti-addictive design.
  No, I can't think what I meant by that, concretely, either.

* Screenshot sharing gallery for Animal Crossing.
  Strong chance that this already exists and is part of the game.

* Lost Property Board for, e.g. school groups and football teams.
  Post "I have lost" / "I found this" notices and attempt matching, communication between users. Geotagging and related search.

* Teletransporter game.
  "Where you can make yourself and animal Mario jumping, weapon, create your own city for refugee and homeless people and it would be infinite."

* GitHub, but for writers.
  Is this wattpad / some fanfic thing already? Leverage the versioning and collaboration somehow.

* A timebank app
  Similar to the sometimes-popular local-only currencies (did Totnes have one?)

* In C++
  Online musical improvisation platform. (Not actually written in C++, that would be taking the joke too far.) Sketch a pattern of notes, share it among friends. when the performance begins you share the patterns, set a tempo / pulse and each of you chooses which, when and at what pitch to play.
  So as not only to annoy Terry Riley, could add a Scratch Orchestra mode based on the Cornelius Cardew-associated project of the early 1970s. There you would sketch a graphical score for the others to interpret.
  Features for this could include video communication, rating of compositions,  AI-linked suggestions for the music (descriptions, prompts?)
  How would the UI for this work? Would have to balance approachability, simplicity and expressiveness.
